Help

The MfN.projektor is an application designed to visualise research and knowledge transfer activities at the Museum für Naturkunde Berlin. It uses the Wiki Forschungsprojekte as a database and to continuously search for connections between previously unconnected activities.

The MfN.projektor currently provides you with two modes of viewing research project data: Cultures and Processes. Cultures allow you to view projects arranged by their departments and funding bodies. Processes show projects and topics arranged by time.

A unique feature of this application are the Discoveries. Here, we utilize a custom-programmed system that uses algorithmic automation to connect research project data from the Wiki Forschungsprojekte. These previously unavailable insights can be shared with colleagues or published to the Wiki for peer discussion.

Cultures

We think that projects, activities, departments and collections are not static. They grow and change, they connect and inspire. We show internal subcultures of our own institution as well as those that connect society and us. To go to the Culture view, select "Cultures" from the menu on the right, select it on the Homepage or click here.

Processes

Processes illustrate the evolving nature of the activities at the Museum für Naturkunde. Currently, we show when projects occur and how topics at our institutions develop over time. To go to the Process view, select "Processes" from the menu on the right, select it on the Homepage or click here.

Discoveries

One of the most exciting features of the application, Discoveries features a curated list of particularly interesting potentials for collaboration, knowledge transfer or competence sharing. To go to the Discoveries view, select "Discoveries" from the menu on the right, select it on the Homepage or click here.

Projects

Projects are displayed as polygons in the visualisations and as diagramms in detail. In detail, you can find the topics of a particular research project, its project lead and many other types of information as well as related Discoveries. To view Projects, select them from one of the visualisations or find a featured one on the Homepage.
